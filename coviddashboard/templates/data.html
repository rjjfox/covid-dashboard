Data

There are hundreds of dashboards being produced all over the world by everyone who can be bothered to make one. With a wealth of potential data sources out there, I chose two that were adequately reliable.

  • UK Government COVID-19 API - timely and granular data is provided through a simple and free-to-use API. The limits to using the API are completely reasonable and the documentation clear enough that it was no problem leveraging this API.
  • WHO - supplying the French side of the data, the WHO produces worldwide health data alongside a beautiful visual dashboard. The WHO provide a CSV download link that can be fed straight into a python script.

Other notable data sources and dashboards

  • Worldometers - this was one of the first places I found personally to see daily and cumulative numbers for cases/deaths across the world. With a seemingly large team working to ensure that the data is correct and trustworthy, this has been my primary source for keeping up to date.
  • Official UK Government Site - not only do they provide an easy to use API but with great documentation, the UK government data team have also produced a display of figures and trends that are exactly what I need to see to understand where we're heading, i.e in or out of lockdown, going up or down a wave. I found this dashboard after I had created my own and subsequently stole a couple of things from it.
  • ECDC - for previous analysis of the COVID-19 pandemic, I have used datasets from the European Centre for Disease Prevention and Control. However, they have lately switched to a weekly reporting schedule which doesn't suit my needs.
  • John Hopkins University - US centric but with great vizualisations of the situation across the world.
  • Our World in Data - Straightforward CSVs updated daily with a wealth of different metrics.
